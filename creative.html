About

Mostly appreciated for her versatility, Charlene has been playing in many bands and ensembles of different style of music from folk to rock, trad from different countries (Irish, Scandinavian, Balkans...).
She started playing violin in Ireland in 2004.
Her Armenian roots led her to playing first Eastern European music. On her journey she met wonderful musicians who introduced her to Scandinavian music. She went then studying music in Sweden at Malung folkhögskola with Kalle Almlöf.
She came back to Ireland to study folk and Irish music. She graduated from the Access Music Project course and Galway Community College course "Music and Performance” with distinctions.
She also graduated from ABRSM in classical music and was an award nominee.
She also participated to different workshops such as Labyrinth, Etno Histeria, or the ones by Con tempo Quartet, lead by great musicians and mentor such as Bogdan Sofei, Giwrgos Papaioannou, Ross Daly, Matija Solce, Nano Stern, Johnny Soling...
Since that time she has been playing different styles of music (folk and trad from many countries) all around Europe, touring in France, Sweden, Slovenia, Italy... Playing gigs and enjoying joining in session inspired her as well as many friends she met on the road.

She also composes music, creates and performs multidisciplinary shows mixing different form of arts (music, storytelling, acroyoga) and has been leading music and artistic workshops both for adult and children.
