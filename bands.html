Bands

  • You, Me, and Clementine

    You, Me, and Clementine are a trio playing Jazz, Folk and Blues.
    Charlene: Violin, Backing Vocals.
    Jenny: Vocals, Ukulele.
    Brian: Bass

  • Fergus McKay & Nothing Concrete

    Fergus McKay and Nothing Concrete is a quintet of musicians coming from different countries and musical background who meet in multicultural spirit.
    It is composed by Fergus McKay (singer, composer and guitar player), Gaia Miato (mandolin, backing vocals) Charlene Donabedian (violin, backing vocals), Justin Jackson (percussions), and Rolland Martinez (double bass).
    This folk/rock band with celtic influences is very festive, friendly and international and has few tricks up its sleeves (such as tap dancing and other surprises:) )

  • JZèle

    Julien Zèle is a singer, songwriter and a guitar player. His music is a mix of different influences such as folk, rock, reggae, ska, techno with french text and a very peculiar voice, a rough diamond. He spent the last 6 years on the road in France and Europe.
    On the way he met Charlène (violin and backing vocals), Jean (drums and percussions), Eve (accordeon) and Keyaki (Didgeridoo). As soon as they me, here they were among artist such as Collectif 13, Soviet Supreme, Luke in the Europopcorn festival 2016.

  • Charlène & Eve's Duo>

    Charlène started to play music 12 years ago in Ireland. Her Armenian roots led her to playing first Eastern European music. On her journey she met wonderful musicians who introduced her to scandinavian music and went then studying in Sweden. She then came back to Ireland and studied in AMP and GCC ans started playing Irish music. Since that time she has been playing different styles of music (folk and trad from many countries) all around Europe, touring in Slovenia, Sweden, France, Italy...Playing gigs, enjoying joining in sessions, playing in festivals and workshops inspired her as well as many friends she met on the road. In Bruxelles she met Eve (graduated in classical guitar from Liège Conservatory and also studying the accordeon)
    Coming from completely different musical background and origins, Eve and Charlene complete each other harmoniously and share their passion for music through this duo, going from Irish music to Balkan music, to trad from here and there.
